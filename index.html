Advice (Especially for beginners!)

  1. Always push yourself and look for new challenges
  2. Call out sexism and unconscious bias, especially in yourself
  3. Seek out people who are different than you; people who study other things than you do
  4. Find mentors who are willing to support you and want to teach you everything they know
  5. Study out of enjoyment, not obligation. Take whatever classes you want while you can
  6. Find a project to work on. It can be open source, an internship, or a side project
  7. Above all else, your health is the most important. Don't put health sacrifices (like all-nighters) on a pedestal. These are generally the result of poor planning and likely result in decreased productivity anyway
  8. Don't let yourself be pushed into less technical positions
  9. Speak loudly and clearly; don't talk over people, but refuse to be talked over
  10. Accept that failure is a part of the learning process
  11. Explore! Find new hobbies, studies, food, people, locaitons, etc
  12. Become an expert googler: Google exceptions, errors, stack traces, buggy or unexpected behavior, CS topics you're confused about, etc

Team Projects

A taste of some of my hackathon projects

Movie Mashups:

Use a markov chain to mash two movie plots together. Use a (nltk) classifier to identify main characters and only use one for maximum mashiness.

Instarecipe:

Uses the instagram API to display recipes.

Pokepon:

Pokepon is a multiplayer game that combines the battle elements of the Pokemon game with the dance and rhythm elements of Patapon (a game sort of like Dance Dance Revolution). Start a battle against your friend and type the battle commands in time with the beat of a song on your Soundcloud playlist. Won the Firebase API award at the PennApps 2013.

Geo.ly

Find yo' friends in yo' browser. Real-time navigation app to find your friends using HTML5 geolocation and the Google Maps API. A compass directs you to your friend's location, while the map updates to show your locations relative to each other.

Boxel

My friends and I dedicated a hackathon to creating a multiplayer Capture the Flag game for the multiplayer server for voxel.js, a minecraft-inspired voxel game built in the browser with Javascript. Implemented team logic and block attributes for gameplay.

Rate My Cat:

Where we get back to what the internet is at its core: cat pics. Won 'Learned the Most' at Hackjam 2013.
